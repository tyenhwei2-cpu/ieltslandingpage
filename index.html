How the practice works

Our AI follows the official IELTS Speaking test structure, with timing, question styles, and interaction patterns designed to mirror the real exam.

Phase 1: Introduction and Overview (4–5 minutes)

  • Style: Casual, rapid-fire Q&A.
  • Execution: The AI asks short questions on 2 everyday topics (e.g., hometown, studies, technology). It pushes you to give concise 2–4 sentence answers.
  • Key feature: The AI will gently interrupt if you speak beyond 25 seconds, training you to stay concise.

Phase 2: The Long Turn (3–4 minutes)

  • Style: Prepared monologue based on a cue card.
  • Execution: You receive a prompt card. You get 60 seconds to take notes, then 2 minutes of uninterrupted speaking.
  • Key feature: The AI remains silent during your monologue. If you stop early, it prompts: "Can you tell me more about that?"

Phase 3: Analytical Discussion (4–5 minutes)

  • Style: Abstract academic debate.
  • Execution: The AI takes the theme from Phase 2 and asks deeper, conceptual questions. It actively challenges simple answers to push your critical thinking.
  • Key feature: Follow-up questions test your ability to speculate, compare, and justify opinions.

Our Grading

We have built a grading system that goes beyond simple feedback. It is designed to be standardized, IELTS-driven, and highly actionable.

  • IELTS rubrics + additional rules: We combine the official IELTS assessment criteria with insights from experienced graders and YouTube examiners.
  • Benchmark grading: Our system gives you a benchmark grade after Phase 1 and 2, then evaluates Phase 3 to measure improvement or regression.
  • Transparent deduction: We explain why each 0.5 mark is deducted from a perfect 9, so you understand the exact gap.
  • Structural and phrase-level feedback: We show you how to improve your English structure and rephrase your answers for higher clarity and impact.
